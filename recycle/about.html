About CLEANSE

Waste management remains prevalent as a major challenge in the Philippines particularly in urban areas like Metro Manila. Improper waste disposal, waste collection efficiency, and the lack of disposal facilities are one of the concerns in the countryâ€™s waste management. About 35, 580 tons of garbage are generated every day in the Philippines. On an average amount, each person induces 0.3 to 0.5 kilograms of garbage every day in urban and rural areas.

The proposed system will add a rewards system together with the implementation of a web- based waste management system. The use of the web-based system as a method of finding the nearest recycling center combines distinctness of ease of use and convenience that will help the user while keeping in mind what will happen next with the waste that they will give away. The study intends to develop a Web-based Waste Management System with a Rewards System to automate the waste management records.

In the Philippines, we may have limited resources to conduct such different waste management plans and solutions. But with the help of technology, we could develop different methods to help us in reducing waste such as developing Web-based waste management systems such as the paper's objective. There is no official Web-based waste management with the reward system in the Philippines used by the common person; the opportunity to create one as the official web-based waste management system is highly rewarding for researchers alike.

CLEANSE is a web-based application created by Frances Ericka Salem, Mark Joseph Fernandez, and Christian Santos from Mapua University for their Capstone Project. This web application is based from a study that uses a web-based system as a method of finding the nearest recycling center or junkshop within the user's area. This application is intended to be an automated waste management record.
